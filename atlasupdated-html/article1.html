The recently concluded Game Awards 2022 was at the top of online discussions when it finally aired. The awards show has been highly publicized and was met with much anticipation during announcements. The reason? The biggest titles in modern video game history are deadlocked for nominations. Elden Rings and God of War: Ragnarok-- the biggest titles in recent history—has set the Awards Show as their battlegrounds. God of War: Ragnarok led the show with a whopping 11 nominations, winning the Best Narrative and Best Action/Adventure Game awards, while lead actor Christopher Judge, who voiced Kratos won Best Performance.

Stray, everyone’s favorite orange tabby cat, won the best indie game award beating titles such as Cult of the Lamb and Neon White. Netflix’s Arcane, which enjoyed online buzz and praises during its release, won the best adaptation against titles such as Sonic the Hedgehog 2, Uncharted, Cyberpunk Edgerunners, and the Cuphead Show. On the Esports and Creators side of the award show, Riot’s Valorant won best Esports against familiar titles CS:GO, Rocket League, DOTA 2, and Riot’s League of Legends. LOUD (Valorant Esports team) won the best Esports team against FaZe Clan and DarkZero Esports. STAGE INTERRUPTION

After Elden Rings was announced as the Game of The Year, an individual followed Elden Ring’s development team to the stage, after Hidetaka Miyazaki finished his speech, the individual approached the mic and said “I want to thank everybody and say I think I want to nominate this award to my Reformed Orthodox rabbi Bill Clinton”. The individual was then escorted and detained by security, an arrest was never made.

The Game Awards dominated online discussions as some were satisfied with the results and some disagreed. The only criticism that was agreeable by all sides was the lack of better Indie representation in the awards show’s list of nominees. Either way, this year’s Game Awards concluded successfully.
